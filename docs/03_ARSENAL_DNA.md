# Section 3: Arsenal DNA - Ball Knowledge System

## What is Arsenal DNA?

**Arsenal DNA** is the comprehensive knowledge engine that understands bowling equipment at the molecular level. It catalogs ball specifications, performance characteristics, layouts, and how different equipment performs in different conditions.

Think of it as your **bowling equipment encyclopedia**—every ball has a DNA profile that reveals:
- Core dynamics
- Cover stock behavior
- RG and differential values
- Performance in specific conditions
- Comparisons to other balls

## Ball Classification System

### By Core Type

**Symmetrical Cores**
- Predictable, smooth reaction
- Easier to control
- Examples: Phaze II, IQ Tour
- Best for: Consistency players

**Asymmetrical Cores**
- More aggressive backend
- More aggressive hook
- Examples: Roto Grip Halo, Hammer Ninja
- Best for: Power players

### By Cover Stock

**Plastic/Polyester**
- Minimal lane friction
- Straight line delivery
- Best for: Spares
- Durability: Excellent

**Urethane**
- Moderate friction
- Smooth hook
- Best for: Light oil conditions
- Durability: Good

**Reactive Resin**
- High friction
- Strong angular motion
- Best for: Medium to heavy oil
- Durability: Good

**Pearl Reactive**
- Very high friction
- Faster hook
- Best for: Oily lanes
- Durability: Good

### By Coverstock Polish

**Polished (Shiny)**
- Reduced friction early
- Longer skid
- Better for: Heavy oil lanes

**Sanded (Dull)**
- Increased friction
- Earlier hook
- Better for: Medium oil lanes

**Matte (Very Dull)**
- Maximum friction
- Earliest hook
- Better for: Dry lanes

## Technical Specifications Reference

### Understanding Ball Numbers

**RG (Radius of Gyration)**
- Range: 2.40 - 2.80
- Lower RG = More hook potential
- Higher RG = Fewer board movement

**Differential (Diff)**
- Range: 0.01 - 0.05
- Higher differential = More aggressive

**Pin Distance**
- How far pin is from center
- Affects hook midway point

**VAL Angle**
- Pin orientation
- Affects hook shape
